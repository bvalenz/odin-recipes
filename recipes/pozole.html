Pozole

Description

Pozole is a traditional Mexian soup often made with pork, hominy, and ancho chilies.

Ingredients

  • ancho chili powder
  • Salt
  • 1 large can of white hominy
  • 3 pounds of pork shoulder
  • 8 cloves of garlic
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons dry oregano

Garnishes

  • 1/2 small cabbage
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 1/2 white onion
  • 2 avocados
  • 4 limes
  • 1 bunch red radishes

Steps

  1. Brown the pork
    2. Heat avocado oil in a large pan on medium high heat. Before cutting the pork, cover it with salt and ancho chili powder. Cut up pork into small pieces and pat them dry with paper towles. Sprinkle them generously with salt. Stir to brown the meat on all sides. Add chopped garlic to the pan right at the end of browning. Let cook with meat for about a minute.
  2. Add pork and spices to a large pot of boilng water
    3. Once the meat has browned, add it to a large pot of boiling water. Add the rised hominy. Add bay leaves, cumin, oregano, and salt.
  3. Cook for 2 to 3 hours until pork is tender
    4. Skim away any excess fat. Add more salt to taste.
  4. Assemble garnishes
    5. As the pozole is cooking, prep the garnishes. Serve them in bowls.
